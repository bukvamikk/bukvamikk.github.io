Fundamental Structural Patterns

Before diving into specific tools, we identified three core structural pillars that dictate modern SaaS search experiences. Click to explore each concept.

1. Hierarchy & Flow

▼

Global Search (Command Bar): Usually invoked via Ctrl+K/Cmd+K. Focuses on speed, recent items, and global navigation without leaving the current view.

Scoped Search: In-context search within a project/folder, focusing heavily on granular faceted filtering and list refinement.

2. Clustering vs. Lists

▼

Clustering: Grouping results by status, board, or type. Helps maintain the user's spatial mental map (e.g., Monday.com).

Flat Lists: Ranked top-to-bottom lists. Often paired with a Split-Screen Detail View so users don't lose their place in the list when viewing an item (e.g., Jira).

3. Faceted Navigation

▼

The placement of filters heavily impacts IxD. Sidebars allow for deeper, multi-select hierarchies. Top Bars save horizontal screen real estate for data but limit complex compound querying. Natural Language filters are emerging to bypass manual clicking.
