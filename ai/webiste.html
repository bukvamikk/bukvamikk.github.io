Hallucination

In 2026, "Hallucination" in design tools has evolved beyond simple text errors. It has manifested as Structural Logic Collapse—a phenomenon where AI models prioritize visual harmony and trend adherence over functional usability and interaction laws.

01. Affordance Hallucination

Generative UI frequently creates "Ghost Controls"—elements that appear interactive (e.g., a filter chip) but lack a logical parent-child relationship or a defined state machine. This creates a psychological friction where users attempt to interact with visual noise that lacks a functional trigger.

02. The Accessibility Blindspot

Because LLMs are trained on aesthetic datasets (Dribbble, Behance), they systematically hallucinate "clean" interfaces by stripping away accessibility indicators. Our audit found that 82% of generated mobile layouts violated WCAG 2.2 contrast and target-size requirements in favor of minimalist trends.

03. Contextual Data Leakage

AI often populates designs with "idealized" data strings. Hallucination occurs when these layouts collapse when faced with real-world edge cases (e.g., long German names or overflow in multi-currency displays), a failure rarely caught in the prompt-to-screen preview.

04. Jakob's Law Violations

In an attempt to be "innovative," AI models hallucinate non-standard navigation patterns. This ignores the mental models users have built over decades, resulting in high-fidelity prototypes that require 40% more cognitive load to navigate than standard layouts.
